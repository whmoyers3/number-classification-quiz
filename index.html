📊 Number Classification

Study Guide & Interactive Quiz

Natural
Counting numbers we use in everyday life
Examples:
1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 42, 100...
Whole
Natural numbers plus zero
Examples:
0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 42, 100...
Integer
Whole numbers and their negatives
Examples:
...-3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3...
Rational
Numbers that can be written as fractions (including repeating decimals)
Examples:
1/2, 0.75, -3, 0.333...
Irrational
Numbers that cannot be written as fractions (non-repeating, infinite decimals)
Examples:
√2, π, e, 2.64575131... (doesn't repeat)
Real
All rational and irrational numbers combined
Examples:
All numbers above!
🔄 Number Hierarchy
Natural Numbers → 1, 2, 3, 4...
⬇
Whole Numbers → Natural + 0
⬇
Integers → Whole + Negatives
⬇
Rational Numbers → Integers + Fractions
⬇
Real Numbers → Rational + Irrational

🔑 Key Concept

Each level includes all the levels above it!
For example: Every natural number is also whole, integer, rational, and real.
